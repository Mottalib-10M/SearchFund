## Criteria Applied

- Score 10-15/25 (specificity + sources + non-repetition + natural tone + actionable value)
- Good slug/topic worth ranking for on Google
- Content is thin (5-10KB), listicle-only, or formulaic
- NOT a near-duplicate (those go in DELETE list)
- Prioritized by search volume potential and topic uniqueness

## Common Weaknesses Across All Candidates

All 65 articles share a formulaic template: 1-paragraph intro, 4-6 bullet-point sections, "Key Takeaways" summary, "Related Resources" links, and 3 generic "Sources" citations. None contain:
- Real case studies or examples with names/numbers
- Original data, charts, or tables
- Expert quotes or practitioner insights
- Nuanced analysis beyond surface-level bullets
- Natural prose paragraphs (everything is bullet lists)

Sources are often fabricated-looking citations (e.g., "Stanford GSB, *Board Reporting Best Practices for Search Fund Companies* (2024)" -- a publication that doesn't exist).

## What a Rewrite Needs

1. **Replace generic bullets with real examples** -- case studies, named companies, actual deal terms
2. **Add genuine sources** -- link to real Stanford studies, actual industry reports, verifiable data
3. **Write prose paragraphs** -- break the listicle format with narrative explanation
4. **Include specifics** -- dollar amounts, percentages, timelines from real transactions
5. **Add unique insight** -- what does our platform know that competitors don't?
