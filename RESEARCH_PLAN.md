## Why original research?

Original research is the single most effective SEO and authority strategy for a niche platform:
- **Backlink magnet**: Academics, journalists, and bloggers cite original data
- **E-E-A-T signal**: Demonstrates firsthand experience and expertise
- **Differentiation**: No competitor publishes original ETA research (only Stanford, IESE, INSEAD)
- **Content flywheel**: Every research piece spawns 5-10 derivative articles, infographics, social posts
- **Lead generation**: Gated PDF downloads build email list

## Research initiative #1 — "The State of European ETA 2026"

**Target publish date**: September 2026 (Q3)
**Format**: Long-form report (50-80 pages) + executive summary web page + downloadable PDF
**URL**: `/en/research/state-of-european-eta-2026`

### Methodology
- **Online survey** distributed to European ETA community (searchers, investors, operators, brokers)
- Target: 150-250 respondents across 15+ countries
- Distribution channels: SearchFundMarket email list, LinkedIn, Searchfunder.com, IESE alumni network, INSEAD alumni network, HEC alumni
- Survey platform: Typeform or Google Forms (free)
- Analysis: descriptive statistics, cross-tabulations by geography/role/experience

### Survey sections
1. **Demographics**: Role (searcher/investor/seller/broker), country, years of ETA experience, MBA school
2. **Search phase**: Duration, capital raised, deal flow sources, number of NDAs signed, number of LOIs submitted
3. **Deal characteristics**: Industry, EBITDA range, purchase price, multiple paid, deal structure (debt/equity/seller note split)
4. **Financing**: Sources used, bank appetite, government programs accessed, typical terms
5. **Post-acquisition**: Revenue growth rate, EBITDA improvement, headcount changes, biggest challenges
6. **Market sentiment**: Where is the market heading? Best countries? Best sectors? Biggest risks?
7. **Compensation**: Search-phase salary, post-acquisition CEO comp, equity received

### Deliverables
- 15-20 charts and data tables
- Country-level breakdowns (France, Germany, UK, Spain, Italy, Nordics, Benelux, Switzerland)
- Year-over-year comparison (baseline for 2027 edition)
- Quotable statistics for press coverage
- Methodology appendix for academic credibility

### Promotion plan
- Press release to M&A and finance media
- Guest post on Searchfunder.com with key findings
- LinkedIn carousel posts (5-7 posts over 2 weeks)
- Email to 50+ ETA newsletters and bloggers
- Submit to SSRN as working paper
- Present findings at IESE Search Fund Conference (if timing aligns)

### Resources needed
- Survey design: 2-3 days
- Distribution and data collection: 4-6 weeks
- Analysis and report writing: 2-3 weeks
- Design and layout: 1 week
- Total: ~3 months from start to publish

## Research initiative #2 — European Search Fund Valuation Benchmarks

**Target publish date**: July 2026 (Q2-Q3)
**Format**: Interactive data page + downloadable PDF
**URL**: `/en/research/valuation-benchmarks`

### Methodology
- **Data compilation** from publicly available sources:
  - Stanford GSB annual search fund studies (1996-2024)
  - IESE International Search Fund Study
  - INSEAD European Search Fund Study
  - Pepperdine Private Capital Markets Report
  - BVR (Business Valuation Resources) DealStats
  - GF Data M&A reports
  - PitchBook and Preqin (public summaries)
- Supplemented by SearchFundMarket survey data (from initiative #1)
- Cross-reference with publicly reported deal terms from press releases, SEC filings

### Data tables to produce
1. **EBITDA multiples by sector** (SaaS, healthcare, manufacturing, professional services, home services, etc.) — with min/25th/median/75th/max
2. **EBITDA multiples by country** (US, UK, France, Germany, Spain, Italy, Netherlands, Nordics, Switzerland)
3. **Revenue multiples by sector** (for businesses with thin/negative EBITDA)
4. **Deal size distribution** (purchase price brackets: <$1M, $1-3M, $3-5M, $5-10M, $10-20M, $20M+)
5. **Capital structure benchmarks** (typical debt/equity/seller note % by deal size)
6. **Time-to-close benchmarks** (LOI to close: 60-120 days typical)
7. **Trends over time** (have multiples expanded? Are deal sizes growing?)

### SEO target
- "EBITDA multiples by industry" (high volume)
- "small business valuation multiples 2026"
- "SME acquisition multiples Europe"
- "search fund deal data"

### Update cadence
- Annual full update after Stanford study release
- Quarterly spot updates when new data sources publish

## Research initiative #3 — European Search Fund Tracker

**Target launch**: June 2026 (Q2), then quarterly updates
**Format**: Live interactive page + quarterly PDF
**URL**: `/en/research/european-search-fund-tracker`

### What to track
- **New search funds launched** in Europe (by country, by quarter)
- **Acquisitions completed** (sector, size, multiple, country)
- **Exits** (type, multiple, hold period, returns)
- **Fundraising activity** (number of funds raised, average size)
- **Key people moves** (new searchers, investor entries, notable hires)

### Data sources
- Public announcements (LinkedIn posts, press releases)
- Searchfunder.com deal announcements
- Company registrations (Companies House UK, Infogreffe France, Handelsregister Germany)
- IESE/INSEAD published deal lists
- Direct submissions from community members (form on site)
- SearchFundMarket platform data (anonymized)

### Format
- Interactive dashboard (filterable by country, sector, year)
- Quarterly narrative summary (500-800 words) analyzing trends
- Downloadable CSV of anonymized deal data
- Year-end comprehensive annual summary

### Competitive advantage
- No one does this systematically for European ETA
- Becomes the go-to reference for journalists, academics, and investors
- Stanford does it for US/global; we own the European angle

## Research initiative #4 — Searcher-CEO Compensation Benchmarks

**Target publish date**: October 2026 (Q4)
**Format**: Data page + report
**URL**: `/en/research/searcher-ceo-compensation`

### Methodology
- Anonymous survey of current and former searcher-CEOs
- Target: 50-100 respondents
- Supplement with Stanford/IESE published compensation data
- Breakdowns by: geography, company size, years as CEO, search model (traditional vs self-funded)

### Data points to collect
1. **Search-phase compensation**: Monthly salary, health insurance, expense budget
2. **Post-acquisition CEO compensation**: Base salary, bonus structure, benefits
3. **Equity economics**: Step-up %, vesting schedule, total equity at entry
4. **Realized economics**: What did searcher-CEOs actually earn at exit? (for those who've exited)
5. **Non-monetary**: Board dynamics, work-life balance, satisfaction scores
6. **By geography**: US vs Europe, within Europe by country
7. **By deal size**: How does comp scale with acquisition size?

### SEO target
- "search fund CEO salary"
- "searcher compensation"
- "ETA compensation"
- "search fund economics"

## Research initiative #5 — Best Performing Search Funds: In-Depth Case Studies

**Target**: Rolling publication, 2 per quarter starting July 2026
**Format**: Long-form articles (3000-5000 words each)
**URL**: `/en/research/case-studies/[slug]`

### Case study selection criteria
- Publicly available information (no NDA material)
- Notable exits (>5x MOIC or strategic significance)
- Geographic diversity (US + European cases)
- Industry diversity
- Permission obtained from searcher-CEO (preferred but not required for public exits)

### Target case studies (Year 1 — 8 total)

| # | Company/Searcher | Why notable | Geography |
|---|-----------------|-------------|-----------|
| 1 | Asurion (Jim Ellis) | Largest search fund exit ever | US |
| 2 | EndoChoice (Mark Gilreath) | Healthcare search fund exit, IPO | US |
| 3 | A European search fund exit | First documented major European exit | EU |
| 4 | A self-funded search success | Demonstrates self-funded model | US/EU |
| 5 | A failed search (pivoted) | Honest failure analysis — rare and valuable | US/EU |
| 6 | A buy-and-build story | Platform + add-ons strategy | US/EU |
| 7 | A female searcher success | Diversity in ETA | US/EU |
| 8 | A cross-border acquisition | European cross-border complexity | EU |

### Structure per case study
1. Background (searcher profile, school, prior experience)
2. Search phase (duration, strategy, challenges)
3. The deal (industry, size, structure, why this company)
4. Post-acquisition (first 100 days, key decisions, growth initiatives)
5. Outcome (exit or current status, returns)
6. Lessons learned (3-5 key takeaways)
7. Sources and references

### Promotion
- Each case study promoted on LinkedIn
- Tagged searcher-CEO for amplification
- Pitched to Searchfunder.com, MBASchooled, etc.
